In the context of the model-driven development of data-centric applications, OCL constraints play a major role in adding precision to the source models (e.g., data models and security models). Several code-generators have been proposed to bridge the gap We propose a model-based methodology to prove the correctness of manually written SQL implementations of OCL constraints in data-centric applications. The approach maps a subset of SQL and OCL into many-sorted first-order logic, enabling SMT solvers to automatically verify correctness. Several examples and supporting tools demonstrate the applicability of the method.
